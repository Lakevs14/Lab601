During a deficit, the goal is often to loose weight while retaining as much muscle mass as possible. To achieve that goal, a 500-1000 daily caloric deficit needs to occur while maintaining adequate protein and fat levels. Protein should be equivilent to your bodyweight in grams while fat should be 30% of your daily caloric intake.
During a surplus, the goal is to gain weight while also attempting to gain the most amount of muscle possible. I personally recommending intaking a surplus of only 100-250 calories daily to prevent as much fat gain as possible. Protein levels should exceed your bodyweight by 20-40 grams for bodybuilding/ powerlifting purposes.
Maintenance is a very interesting subject in terms of bodybuilding/ powerlifting. My ongoing theory is that it is possible to gain muscle and loose fat at the same time while in a maintenance. Unfortunately, those in the very advance stages of both sports need to be in a surplus to gain muscle. For competition purposes to make weight, both sports will often dehydrate themselves considering that water weight contributes to our body weight significantly.
